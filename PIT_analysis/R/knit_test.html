John Day Steelhead Overshoot

Bonneville AFF PIT tagging summary

Summer 2020

Research Brief

A collaborative research effort with the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife (ODFW) and Oregon
State University (OSU), aiming to better understand individual adult migrations of John Day River
origin summer steelhead in the Columbia River between Bonneville and McNary Dams. The main
emphasis for this research targets the overshoot and fallback phenomenon, where the majority of John
Day origin steelhead overshoot the mouth of the John Day River during their upstream migration, and
continue upstream ascending McNary Dam before falling back and returning to the John Day River
later in the year.

Methods

Adult steelhead were sampled inside the adult fish facility at Bonneville Dam during the 2020 summer
field season in accordance to Appendix G of the 2017 Fish Passage Plan. 200 adult steelhead were
inserted with PIT tags between July 13th - September 18th
